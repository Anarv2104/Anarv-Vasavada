There is a version of building that looks like building but is not.

It involves a lot of Twitter threads about what you are working on. Weekly newsletters about your progress. LinkedIn posts about lessons learned. Podcast appearances about your journey.

The work itself is secondary. The performance of the work is primary.

## What I noticed

When I started building Inflion, I did not tell anyone. I just built it. When it was working, I published it to PyPI. That was the announcement.

No build-in-public thread. No journey posts. Just the thing itself, shipped, available, usable.

Something interesting happened. The work spoke for itself in a way that no thread about the work could have.

## The compounding difference

Announcing what you are building creates a social debt. You owe people an update. You owe them the completed version. The pressure to deliver shifts from internal — wanting the thing to be good — to external — wanting to not disappoint people who are watching.

That shift corrupts the work. You optimize for shipping something rather than shipping something good. You cut corners that would have been obvious if you were only answerable to yourself.

Building in silence removes that pressure entirely. The only audience is the work itself.

## This is not hiding

Building in silence is not the same as building in isolation. I talk to people about what I am working on — people who can give me genuine feedback, catch mistakes, push the thinking further.

The difference is I am not performing for them. I am thinking with them.

Public building is performance. Private building with the right people is collaboration. The output is very different.

## When to speak

The right time to talk about the work is when the work can speak for itself.

Not when you have an idea. Not when you have a prototype. When you have something real that someone can use, evaluate, or engage with directly.

At that point, you are not performing building. You are showing what was built. That is a different thing entirely — and it lands differently.

## What this costs

Building in silence is harder than it sounds. There is no external validation to keep you going through the difficult parts. No one cheering you on. No metrics on your posts to tell you the idea matters.

You have to believe it matters based on your own judgment. That is a harder thing to sustain, and it requires a different relationship with your own work.

But the work that comes out the other side is cleaner. Less contaminated by what people want to hear. More honest about what the problem actually is.

That is the trade. I think it is worth it.
